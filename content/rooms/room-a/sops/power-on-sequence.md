# Power-On and Shutdown Sequence

Getting the studio powered up and shut down properly. The order matters — doing it wrong can send loud pops through the speakers or damage equipment.

The golden rule: **speakers on last, off first.**

## Power-On Sequence

### 1. Lights

Turn on the studio lights. (Reminder: the hallway lights are outside the studio door.)

### 2. Power conditioner (bottom of rack)

Flip the switch on the bottom power conditioner. This single switch powers up:

- All preamps (1073s, API 312s, Pacifica, Psytech)
- All outboard gear
- The MIDI interface
- The synths (most of them)

**Things that stay OFF by default** — these are left off because they make noise even when you're not using them:

- **Oberheim Matrix 1000s** — they have a mechanical noise. Only turn them on when you're actually going to play them.
- **Yamaha SY-77** — emits a high-frequency noise when powered. Turn it on only when you need it.
- **Sabatron tube preamp** — has a barely audible hum. Fine to leave off unless you're tracking through it.

### 3. Euphonics controller

This one doesn't come on with the power conditioner. It has a soft power button tucked behind its mounting bracket. Reach behind and press it. It's a little tricky to find the first time.

### 4. Computer (Mac Mini)

Head to the computer closet and power on the Mac Mini. Check your onboarding document for the login password. Give it a minute to boot up and load everything.

### 5. Monitors — LAST

The Genelec monitors go on **last**. This is the most important part of the sequence.

Here's the thing about the Genelecs: the power buttons are on the back of each speaker, tucked behind the mounting brackets. They're a little tricky to reach. Feel around behind the bracket — the button is in the same spot on both speakers (they're identical, not mirrored).

Press the power button on each monitor. Start with the volume down on the MYO console, then bring it up slowly once both monitors are on.

## Shutdown Sequence

Shutdown is the reverse. **Monitors off first.**

### 1. Monitors — FIRST

Turn the monitor volume all the way down on the MYO console. Then power off each Genelec (same buttons behind the brackets).

### 2. Computer

Shut down the Mac Mini properly through the OS. Don't just yank the power.

### 3. Euphonics controller

Press the soft power button behind the bracket to power it off.

### 4. Power conditioner

Flip the power conditioner switch off. This kills everything else in one go.

### 5. Turn off any exceptions you turned on

If you powered on the Matrix 1000s, SY-77, or Sabatron during your session, make sure they're off too.

### 6. Arm the alarm and lock up

Follow the studio access SOP for the lockup procedure. Check your onboarding document for the alarm code.

## Why the order matters

When you turn on a power amp or active monitor before the source equipment is ready, it can pick up transient voltage spikes and turn them into very loud pops through the speakers. Over time, this damages the drivers. Same thing in reverse — if you kill the source gear while the monitors are still on, the sudden signal drop can pop the speakers.

"Speakers on last, off first" is the single most important habit in any studio.
