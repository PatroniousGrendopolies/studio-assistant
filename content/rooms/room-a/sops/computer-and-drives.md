# Computer and Drives

The studio runs on a Mac Mini tucked away in the computer closet. Here's what you need to know about the computer, storage, and connections.

## The Mac Mini

The Mac Mini lives in the computer closet (not at the desk). Power it on as part of the startup sequence. Check your onboarding document for the login password.

### Connections

The Mac Mini has several cables running to it — if someone used their own laptop before your session, they may have unplugged some of these. Double-check that everything is connected:

- **Ethernet** — internet access
- **Lightning** — storage drives and UAD interface
- **HDMI** — video to the monitor at the desk
- **USB** — keyboard, mouse, and MIDI interface

If something isn't working (no internet, no drives showing up, no screen), check these connections first. Reconnecting a cable usually solves it.

## Storage drives

### Working drive (6TB spinning)

This is where you'll do most of your work. It's a 6TB spinning drive — plenty fast for tracking and overdubbing. Unless you're doing a massive session (20+ simultaneous inputs) or loading heavy sample libraries, this drive handles everything fine.

**Each client gets a named folder on the working drive.** Create your project folder here if one doesn't already exist. Keep it organized — future you will appreciate it.

### SSD tracking drive

For high-track-count sessions (20+ inputs recording simultaneously) or when you're loading large sample libraries (orchestral templates, drum libraries, etc.), use the SSD. It has the throughput to handle the demand without hiccups.

### Backup drive (8TB)

The 8TB drive is for backups. Back up your session files here before you leave. Don't use it as a working drive — it's meant for redundancy, not active recording.

## Software

### DAWs

- **Pro Tools** — installed and ready to go
- **Ableton Live** — installed and ready to go

Make sure the DAW's audio engine is pointed at the Metric Halo interface (not the Mac's built-in audio). Check the audio settings if you're not getting signal.

### Tidal

Tidal is installed for reference listening — lossless quality. Great for A/B-ing your mix against references. Use it through the monitors for critical listening.

### Oracle X

Oracle X handles MIDI routing between the synths, controllers, and the DAW. See the synths and MIDI SOP for details.

## Tips

- If the Mac Mini is unresponsive, check the HDMI cable first — sometimes it just needs to be reseated.
- Don't install software on the studio computer without checking with Nigel first.
- Always save your session to the working drive, then back it up to the 8TB drive before you leave.
- If you used your own laptop during the session, reconnect all the Mac Mini cables before you go. The next person will thank you.
